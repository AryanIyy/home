Global Warming: Eight Facts

By: Aryan Iyyappan

Eight Fun Facts about Global Warming:

  • Since 1870, global sea levels have risen by about 8 inches

  • There is more carbon dioxide in the atmosphere today than at any point in the last 800,000 years

  • Greenhouse gases that collect in the atmosphere are like a thickening blanket, trapping the sun's heat and causing the planet to increase it's warmth

  • Global warming puts coral reefs in danger as the ocean warms

  • Heat waves caused by global warming present greater risk of heat-related illness and death

  • Americans make up just 4% of the Earth's population, but they produce 25% of the carbon dioxide pollution

  • With the start of industry in the 1700's, humans began emitting fossil fuels from coal, oil, and gas to run vehicles and factories.

  • There is a decive that is inexpensive and can convert CO2 to oxygen without our care, trees
